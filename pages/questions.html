What are the types of questions that can be asked in German?

There are two types that can be asked in German (and in most languages). We can either ask yes/no questions or specific questions by utilizing question words.

Question Word Questions

Here are the question words used in German:

  • Wann (when)
  • Wo (where)
  • Was (what)
  • Wer (wen, wem) (who)
  • Warum (wieso, welhalb) (why)
  • Wie (how)
  • Welch (which)

How does the structure of questions differ between English and German?

English: [Question Word] [Verb 1] [Subject] [Optional Verb 2] [Remainder of the Sentence]?


German: [Question Word] [Verb 1] [Subject] [Remainder of the Sentence] [All Remaining Verbs]?

Verb 1 in the English example can only be a modal or a helper verb. This is not the case in German, instead any verb can come after the question word.

Wann (when)

Wann is the German word to ask for time.

Wann kommst du nach Hause?

When are you coming home?

There are two important variations to note.

(Since when)

Seit wann wohnst du hier?

Since when have you been living here?

(Until when)

Bis wann musst du arbeiten?

Until when/what time do you have to work?

Something to note...

There is a similar word in German (wenn) that is used to indicate a specific time for something.

Yes and No Questions

These are questions that can be answered fully with a simple yes or no.

They are formed by placing the main verb of the sentence at the front. For example we can turn the statment: Ich renne oft. (I run often) into a question by placing the verb at the front and changing the pronoun into second person: Rennst du oft? (Do you run often?).
